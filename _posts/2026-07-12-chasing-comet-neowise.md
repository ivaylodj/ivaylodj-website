In July 2020, for the first time in decades, a comet bright enough to see with the naked eye crossed our skies: C/2020 F3, NEOWISE.

I photographed it over several evenings from around the Cape Kaliakra fortress and the Stone Forest near Varna, in Northeastern Bulgaria — sometimes after sunset, sometimes just before dawn, as it climbed away from the horizon. Some nights were crowded, others solitary; all of them calm and clear.

Technically it was forgiving compared to deep-sky work: a wide lens and a few seconds at a high enough ISO were enough to catch both tails. The hard part was timing and clear skies.

By August it had faded, on its way back out for another ~6,800 years — a reminder that some subjects you only get one chance at, and a lifetime memory.
