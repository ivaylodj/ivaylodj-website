The best nightscapes start long before dark. I check the moon phase, the forecast, and a light-pollution map, then drive out to my favourite dark-sky spots near Varna where the Milky Way still shows clearly.

## Gear and settings

My setup is simple: a Sony A7 III, a fast wide lens at f/1.8, ISO 3200, and exposures around 13–15 seconds on a sturdy tripod. The rule of thumb is to keep the stars as points, not trails — and lately I've started using a star tracker for longer frames.

## Composition

Composition matters as much as settings. I look for a foreground that anchors the sky — a lone tree, a ruin, the calm or stormy coastline — so the image is a landscape *with* stars, not just a star chart.

## Processing

Back home, processing is about restraint: lift the core of the galaxy, control the noise, and keep the colours honest. I used to work in a stripped-down Sony edition of Capture One, then Sequator and GIMP; more recently I've moved my whole post-processing workflow to entirely free and open-source software.

## If you're starting out

Dress warmer than you think, bring a red torch, and be patient. The sky rewards the people who wait.
