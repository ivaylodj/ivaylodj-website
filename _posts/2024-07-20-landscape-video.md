## About This Video

Time-lapse photography compresses hours or days of footage into seconds, revealing the passage of time and the subtle changes in light, weather, and atmosphere that the human eye typically misses.

## Behind the Scenes

Creating compelling time-lapse sequences requires:

- **Equipment**: Stable tripod, intervalometer or camera with built-in interval timer
- **Planning**: Scout location, determine shooting duration, calculate interval spacing
- **Settings**: Manual exposure mode (to avoid flicker), RAW format (if possible), fast SD cards
- **Post-Production**: Import sequence into video editor, set frame rate (typically 24fps), color grade for consistency

The result is a cinematic narrative that transforms a static landscape into a dynamic story of light, shadow, and movement.

---

**Pro Tip**: For a 1-hour sequence at 24fps, take one image every 2-3 seconds. This equals roughly 1,200-1,800 exposures for a 60-second video.
