## Gallery intros

**Nightscapes** *(slider)*
Long after the last light fades, a different landscape emerges. These nightscapes trace the Milky Way as it arcs over the Bulgarian countryside and the Black Sea coast the dark - quiet skies that reward patience and a steady tripod. Some frames are a single exposure held open to gather starlight the eye alone can't see, while others are stacked to perfect the faint lights of the starry night. The frames are shot around Cape Kaliakra, Bolata cove, Kamen Bryag and Yaylata National Reserve, Cape Emine and other prominent dark-sky spots located mostly in Northeastern Bulgaria.]

**Comet NEOWISE** *(slider)*
In July 2020, comet C/2020 F3 (NEOWISE) came close enough to see unaided - a visitor that won't return for thousands of years. I photographed it from around Cape Kaliakra fortress and it's surroundings as it hung above the horizon at dusk, its pale tail stretched across the northern sky. A fleeting subject, gone for lifetimes.

**Sunsets** *(grid)*
Golden hour is the day's last and most generous light. This collection follows the sun down to the horizon, skies turning from amber to deep crimson over the Black Sea and the beatiful city of Varna]. No two evenings are alike - these are the ones worth standing still for.

**Sunrises** *(slider)*
There's a particular stillness in the hour before the world wakes. These sunrises were made in the cold blue quiet of early morning, waiting for the first warm light to break over the Black Sea east horizon. The reward for the early alarm.

**Birds & Wildlife** *(grid)*
Wildlife photography is a lesson in patience. These frames catch birds and other wild subjects in their own element - in flight, at rest, on the hunt, mainly around Varna and the Northeastern Bulgarian coast and wetlands. No staging, no baiting: just long waits and quiet distance.

**Various** *(grid)*
A gathering of images that don't belong to a single series - moments caught in passing, experiments, and favourites that resist tidy categories. The sketchbook of the work: textures, details, studies in light.

**Vera Su** *(slider)*
In September 2021, the cargo ship *Vera Su* ran aground on Bulgaria's northern Black Sea coast near Kamen Bryag at the center of the Yaylata Protected area. For weeks the stranded vessel became an unlikely landmark - a study in scale, weather, and the slow drama of salvage. A documentary record of a ship held fast against the shore.

**Day of Varna, 2019** *(grid)*
Varna marks its city day on 15 August with music, crowds, and colour along the seafront. Street photography from 2019 - candid faces and the easy energy of a city at its most alive.

**Day of Varna, 2020** *(grid)*
The same celebration in a strange year. 2020's Day of Varna was quieter - masks and distance threaded through the familiar rhythm of the seafront, but the city's spirit held.

**Funfair, Varna — Summer** *(slider)*
The Varna funfair in high summer: neon, motion, and the particular joy of a warm evening at the fair. Long exposures dissolve the spinning rides into ribbons of light.

**Funfair, Varna — Winter** *(grid)*
The same fairground under winter's cold light - quieter and starker, the machinery of fun at rest / lit against an early dark]. A deliberate counterpoint to the summer set of images.

**Spring** *(slider)*
Spring around Varna, Northeastern Bulgaria - the land waking in fresh green and soft light, blossom and new growth. The season of beginnings, and of long, forgiving golden hours.

**Autumn** *(grid)*
Autumn's turning palette - warm reds and golds across the Northeastern Bulgaria region's woodlands and coast. The year's most painterly light, and its most fleeting.

**Namibia 2021** *(grid)*
Namibia is a landscape of extremes - the red dunes of Sossusvlei, the Skeleton Coast, endless desert horizons. Photographed in June 2021, this series follows light across one of Earth's oldest, emptiest places, where scale humbles and colour astonishes.

**Morocco** *(slider)*
From the medinas of ancient cities to the dunes of the Sahara and the Atlas mountains, Morocco is colour and texture in constant motion. A travel series chasing the interplay of architecture, landscape, and everyday life.

**Rome** *(slider)*
The eternal city, photographed in September 2019 - ancient stone, golden façades, and the light that has drawn artists for centuries. Rome frames itself around every corner; these are a wanderer's favourites.

**La Ciotat** *(slider)*
La Ciotat, on France's Mediterranean coast - the old harbour, the calanques overlooking Cassis, the soft southern light near the town where cinema itself was born. A quiet Provençal counterpoint to the grand cities.

### Post 1 "How I Photograph the Milky Way from Bulgaria"
*Suggested frontmatter:* template `blog_standard` (or `blog_gallery` if you add photos); categories `["Nightscapes","How-To"]`; tags `["astrophotography","milky way","nightscape","bulgaria","tutorial"]`; cover `img/photos/Nightscapes/img-1.jpg`.

Draft body:
> The best nightscapes start long before dark. I check the moon phase, the forecast, and a light-pollution map, then drive out to my favorites go-to dark-sky spots near Varna where the Milky Way still shows clearly.
>
> My setup is simple: sony a7iii camera, a fast wide lens at f/1.8, ISO 3200, and exposures around 13-15 seconds on a sturdy tripod. The rule of thumb is to keep stars as points, not trails. Recently, I started to use a star tracker for longer frames]as well.
>
> Composition matters as much as settings. I look for a foreground that anchors the sky - a lone tree, a ruin, the calm or stormy coastline - so the image is a landscape *with* stars, not just a star chart.
>
> Back home, processing is about restraint: lift the core of the galaxy, control the noise, and keep the colours honest. In the past I have used to use sony stripped down C1 edition, sequator and then GIMP for the edits, but more recently I switched my post processing workflow to use entirely FOSS software.
>
> If you're starting out: dress warmer than you think, bring a red torch, and be patient. The sky rewards the people who wait.
